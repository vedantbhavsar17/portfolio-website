How My Machine Learning Journey Started

Published on: July 23, 2025

Introduction


Everyone has that one spark a moment, a question, or a curiosity that leads them onto a completely new path. For me, that path was machine learning. I'm still a beginner, still learning, still figuring it out, but here's how it all began and why I'm sticking with it.


My Background: Where It All Began


I’m currently pursuing BCA in Web Development at SDJ International College in Surat. From the very start of my course, I found myself drawn toward coding. Even before college began, I was learning C++ on my own. That early exposure gave me a head start and ignited my curiosity to go deeper into the world of programming.

Once college started, I got introduced to C programming, and that helped me build a strong foundation. Around the same time, I began learning HTML and CSS through YouTube. I even created a simple demo site modeled after Amazon — just to test what I could build with my own hands. These small wins kept me excited. But then, I came across something even more fascinating.


How I Got Curious About Machine Learning


While exploring tech content online, I started experimenting with various AI tools like OpenAI and Gemini etc. Watching how these tools understood and responded like humans. I kept asking myself:

How are they doing this? What’s going on behind the scenes?

That led me down the rabbit hole of research, where I discovered terms like machine learning (ML), AI, and natural language processing (NLP). The more I learned, the more curious I became. I wanted to understand how machines learn, how models are trained, and how data drives everything.
I found a few really good YouTube channels especially CampusX, whose DSMP (Data Science Mentorship Program) gave me a proper roadmap. That was the real beginning of my ML journey.


Why I Chose Machine Learning


Initially, I was afraid of the math involved in ML. I didn’t think I could handle it. But when I started practicing solving problems and learning concepts my confidence started growing. I realized that math isn’t the enemy it’s the language of machine learning.

As I went deeper, I fell in love with concepts like:

  • Hypothesis testing
  • Probability
  • PDF & PMF
  • Data distributions
  • Vectors & Tensors

Today, math is actually my favorite subject. I enjoy breaking down complex ideas and truly understanding them not just for marks or exams, but to build something meaningful.



My First Steps into ML Concepts


Before jumping into advanced ML, I focused on building my Python skills. I’m still learning Python now, and so far I’ve covered:


  • OOP (Object-Oriented Programming)
  • Loops and iterations
  • Generators (which are super useful in ML and DL)
  • NumPy and Pandas

Understanding how these tools work is like preparing my toolkit for machine learning. I now realize that everything from data handling to model building rests on a solid understanding of these basics.


Final Words: My ML Journey So Far


For me, machine learning isn’t just a tech skill it’s a personal transformation. I started as someone unsure about math, and now I enjoy learning probability and statistics. I started as a web dev student, and now I’m dreaming in tensors and models.


If I had to describe my ML journey in one line, it would be:


"I’m not an expert - I’m just someone who chose to begin and never stopped learning."

And this journey?
It’s only just getting started.
